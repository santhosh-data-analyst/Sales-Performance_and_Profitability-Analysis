
# KPI Definitions & Business Interpretation

This document defines the KPIs used across the dashboard and explains their business relevance.

---

## 1. Total Revenue

**Definition:**  
Sum of all sales transactions.

**Business Meaning:**  
Represents topline performance but does not indicate profitability.

**Used On:**  
Page 1 – Sales Performance Overview

---

## 2. Total Profit

**Definition:**  
Total revenue minus associated costs.

**Business Meaning:**  
Indicates overall financial health.

**Used On:**  
All pages

---

## 3. Profit Margin %

**Definition:**  
Profit as a percentage of revenue.

**Business Meaning:**  
Measures efficiency of pricing and cost control.

**Used On:**  
All pages, especially profitability analysis

---

## 4. Average Discount %

**Definition:**  
Weighted average discount applied across sales.

**Business Meaning:**  
Shows pricing discipline and discount aggressiveness.

**Used On:**  
Page 3 – Customer & Discount Profitability Analysis

---

## 5. Revenue at Risk

**Definition:**  
Revenue generated by loss-making customers or products.

**Business Meaning:**  
Highlights exposure to unprofitable sales volume.

**Used On:**  
Page 3 – KPI Summary

---

## 6. Profit Leakage

**Definition:**  
Total negative profit generated from unprofitable sales.

**Business Meaning:**  
Direct indicator of value destruction.

**Used On:**  
Page 3 – KPI Summary

---

## 7. Customers in Profit Leakage Zone

**Definition:**  
Count of customers operating below break-even.

**Business Meaning:**  
Shows scale of pricing or cost issues at customer level.

**Used On:**  
Page 3 – KPI Summary

---

## 8. Customer Action Categories

### Protect
- Low discount
- Positive margin
- Pricing discipline is working

### Monitor
- High discount
- Still profitable
- Discount tightening opportunity

### Renegotiate
- Low discount
- Negative margin
- Structural cost or pricing issue

### Drop
- High discount
- Negative margin
- Severe profit leakage

**Business Meaning:**  
Provides actionable customer-level decision framework.

---

## Final Note
KPIs are designed to support **decision-making**, not vanity reporting.
Each metric ties directly to profitability, pricing discipline, or risk exposure.
